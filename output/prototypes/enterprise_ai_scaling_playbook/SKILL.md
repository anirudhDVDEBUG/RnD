# Enterprise AI Scaling Playbook

A structured approach to scaling AI across the enterprise — moving from isolated experiments to compounding, organization-wide impact through trust, governance, workflow design, and quality at scale.

## When to use

- "How do we scale AI adoption across our enterprise?"
- "We need an AI governance framework for our organization"
- "How do we move from AI experiments to production at scale?"
- "What's the playbook for enterprise AI maturity?"
- "How do we maintain AI quality and trust as we scale?"

## How to use

### Phase 1: Build Trust with Early Wins

1. **Identify high-impact, low-risk use cases** — Start with internal productivity tools, document summarization, or customer support augmentation where AI can demonstrate clear value without critical risk.
2. **Measure and communicate results** — Track time saved, accuracy improvements, and user satisfaction. Share wins broadly to build organizational confidence.
3. **Establish feedback loops** — Create channels for users to report issues, suggest improvements, and share successful patterns.

### Phase 2: Establish Governance

1. **Define an AI governance framework** covering:
   - Data privacy and security policies
   - Model evaluation and approval processes
   - Usage monitoring and audit trails
   - Risk classification for different use case tiers
   - Responsible AI principles and ethical guidelines
2. **Create cross-functional AI governance committees** with representatives from legal, security, engineering, and business units.
3. **Set clear boundaries** — Define what AI can and cannot be used for, and establish escalation paths for edge cases.

### Phase 3: Design Scalable Workflows

1. **Map existing workflows** to identify where AI delivers the highest leverage — look for repetitive, time-consuming tasks with clear quality criteria.
2. **Design human-in-the-loop patterns** — Determine the right level of human oversight for each workflow tier:
   - **Tier 1 (Full autonomy):** Low-risk, easily reversible tasks (e.g., drafting internal summaries)
   - **Tier 2 (Review before action):** Medium-risk tasks requiring human approval (e.g., customer-facing content)
   - **Tier 3 (AI-assisted):** High-risk tasks where AI supports but humans decide (e.g., financial analysis, compliance)
3. **Standardize AI integration patterns** — Create reusable templates, prompts, and integration points so teams don't reinvent the wheel.
4. **Build shared prompt libraries and evaluation datasets** to ensure consistency across teams.

### Phase 4: Maintain Quality at Scale

1. **Implement continuous evaluation** — Set up automated quality checks, regression testing, and output monitoring.
2. **Track key metrics:**
   - Output accuracy and consistency
   - User adoption and satisfaction rates
   - Time-to-value for new use cases
   - Incident rates and resolution times
3. **Create centers of excellence** — Designate AI champions in each business unit to share best practices, troubleshoot issues, and drive adoption.
4. **Iterate on governance** — Review and update policies quarterly as capabilities and use cases evolve.

### Phase 5: Compound Impact

1. **Connect AI-powered workflows** — Look for opportunities where the output of one AI workflow feeds into another, creating compounding value.
2. **Invest in custom fine-tuning and tooling** for your highest-value use cases.
3. **Share learnings across the organization** through internal case studies, workshops, and communities of practice.
4. **Measure organizational AI maturity** and set targets for advancement.

## Key Principles

- **Trust is the foundation** — Without organizational trust, scaling stalls. Invest in transparency, explainability, and consistent quality.
- **Governance enables speed** — Clear guardrails let teams move faster, not slower. Good governance removes ambiguity.
- **Start narrow, scale wide** — Prove value in focused areas before expanding. Depth before breadth.
- **Quality over quantity** — Ten well-designed AI workflows beat a hundred poorly implemented ones.
- **People drive adoption** — Technology alone doesn't scale. Champions, training, and cultural readiness are essential.
