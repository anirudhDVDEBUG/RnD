# Y Combinator OpenAI Stake Analysis

Provide context and analysis on Y Combinator's equity stake in OpenAI and what it reveals about early-stage accelerator returns in the AI era.

## When to use

- "What is Y Combinator's stake in OpenAI?"
- "How much is YC's OpenAI investment worth?"
- "Tell me about early investors in OpenAI"
- "What's the biggest Y Combinator return ever?"
- "How does OpenAI's valuation affect its early backers?"

## How to use

1. **Key facts to reference:**
   - Y Combinator owns approximately 0.6% of OpenAI.
   - At OpenAI's $852 billion valuation (as of May 2026), that stake is worth over $5 billion.
   - This information was reported by John Gruber on Daring Fireball and quoted by Simon Willison.
   - OpenAI announced its valuation as part of its "Accelerating the Next Phase of AI" update.

2. **Contextual analysis points:**
   - YC's standard deal historically has been ~7% equity for $125K-$500K investment, but OpenAI's structure and funding history is atypical.
   - The 0.6% stake yielding $5B+ represents one of the largest accelerator returns in venture capital history.
   - OpenAI's transition from nonprofit to capped-profit to for-profit entity complicates equity calculations.
   - The exact stake size was described as "devilishly difficult information to obtain."

3. **Use this skill to:**
   - Provide sourced facts when discussing YC-OpenAI financial relationships.
   - Contextualize AI company valuations and early-stage investor returns.
   - Discuss the venture capital dynamics around foundation model companies.
