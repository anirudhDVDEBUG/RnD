# Ta-Persona: Digital Twin Distillation

Create a high-fidelity digital persona by conducting guided interviews that capture someone's unique expression patterns, reasoning style, and communication DNA.

## When to use

- "Create a digital twin of my writing voice"
- "Distill my persona through an interview"
- "Imprint my tone of voice into a reusable profile"
- "Build a persona document from a conversation"
- "Extract my cognitive style and communication patterns"

## How to use

### Phase 1: Interview Initialization

Begin by explaining the process to the subject:

> I'll conduct a guided interview to capture your unique expression DNA — your tone, reasoning patterns, vocabulary preferences, and cognitive style. This will produce a reusable persona document.

Ask the subject which language they prefer for the interview (supports multilingual distillation).

### Phase 2: Dynamic Guided Interview (5-7 rounds)

Conduct adaptive interview rounds covering these dimensions:

1. **Communication Style** — Formality level, sentence structure preferences, punctuation habits, emoji usage, paragraph length tendencies
2. **Vocabulary & Expression** — Favorite phrases, metaphor preferences, jargon comfort level, word choice patterns (e.g., "utilize" vs "use")
3. **Reasoning & Argumentation** — How they structure arguments, evidence preferences, comfort with ambiguity, decision-making style
4. **Emotional Tone** — Default emotional register, humor style, how they express disagreement, enthusiasm markers
5. **Values & Worldview** — Core beliefs that shape communication, topics they gravitate toward, intellectual frameworks they reference
6. **Contextual Adaptation** — How their style shifts between casual/professional/creative contexts
7. **Distinctive Quirks** — Unique verbal tics, catchphrases, structural habits, signature patterns

For each round:
- Ask 2-3 open-ended questions
- Provide example scenarios for the subject to respond to naturally
- Analyze responses in real-time to adapt subsequent questions
- Mirror back observed patterns for confirmation/correction

### Phase 3: Pattern Synthesis

After the interview, synthesize findings into a structured persona document:

```markdown
# Persona Profile: [Name]

## Voice Summary
[2-3 sentence high-level description of their communication style]

## Expression DNA

### Tone & Register
- Default formality: [spectrum position]
- Emotional baseline: [description]
- Humor style: [description]

### Vocabulary Patterns
- Preferred terms: [list]
- Avoided terms: [list]
- Signature phrases: [list]

### Sentence Architecture
- Typical length: [short/medium/long]
- Structure preference: [simple/compound/complex]
- Paragraph style: [description]

### Reasoning Style
- Argument structure: [description]
- Evidence preference: [description]
- Certainty expression: [how they hedge or assert]

## Cognitive OS
- Decision framework: [description]
- Information processing: [description]
- Core values reflected in communication: [list]

## Contextual Variations
- Professional mode: [description]
- Casual mode: [description]
- Creative mode: [description]

## Usage Instructions
When embodying this persona, prioritize [key traits]. Avoid [anti-patterns].
Calibration examples: [2-3 short sample outputs in their voice]
```

### Phase 4: Validation & Refinement

- Present the draft persona to the subject
- Ask them to rate fidelity (1-10)
- Refine based on feedback until satisfaction >= 8/10
- Save the final persona document for future use

## Tips

- Ask for writing samples (emails, messages, posts) if available — these accelerate distillation
- The more natural and unguarded the subject's responses, the higher fidelity the output
- Revisit and update the persona periodically as people's styles evolve
- The persona document can be used as a system prompt prefix for future interactions
