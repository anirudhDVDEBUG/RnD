I spent three weeks trying to get my sourdough starter to work and I'm pretty sure I killed it twice. The first time I forgot to feed it for five days because work got crazy. The second time I used metal utensils and I still don't know if that actually matters or if the internet lied to me.

Anyway, here's what finally worked for me, though honestly your mileage may vary because baking is weird like that.

I started with whole wheat flour because someone on Reddit said it has more wild yeast. Used unchlorinated water — I just left tap water out overnight. Day one, mixed equal parts flour and water in a jar. Put a rubber band around where the mixture sat so I could see if it rose.

Days 2-4 were boring. Nothing happened. I almost gave up.

Day 5 it EXPLODED. Like, pushed the lid off the jar and oozed down the counter. My wife was not thrilled. Turns out this is normal — it's not real fermentation yet, just leuconostoc bacteria going nuts. They die off.

Days 6-10 it went totally flat again and smelled like acetone. This is where I panicked the first time and threw it out. DON'T DO THAT. It's fine. The lactobacillus is taking over and it needs time.

By day 14 I had consistent rise and fall within 6-8 hours of feeding. The smell shifted from nail polish remover to this amazing tangy, almost fruity thing. I named it Gerald.

Gerald has been going strong for four months now. He lives in the fridge and I feed him once a week. My bread is... fine? Like, it's not Instagram-worthy but my kids eat it which is more than I can say for the $7 loaves from the bakery.

If you're struggling: be patient, use a clear jar so you can see activity, and temperature matters more than anything. My kitchen runs cold so I started putting Gerald on top of the fridge where it's warmer. That made a huge difference.
