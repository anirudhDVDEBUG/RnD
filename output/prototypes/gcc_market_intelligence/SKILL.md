# GCC Market Intelligence

Open skill for B2B/B2G founders entering the GCC — Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman.

## When to use

- "How do I enter the Saudi market as a B2B SaaS company?"
- "What sovereign wealth funds should I target in the GCC?"
- "Walk me through GCC procurement and B2G sales processes"
- "What localization and compliance requirements exist for UAE and Saudi?"
- "Help me build a soft-landing strategy for the Gulf region"

## How to use

### 1. Identify target market and segment

Clarify which GCC country (or countries) the user is targeting and whether the go-to-market is B2B, B2G, or both. Key considerations:

- **Saudi Arabia (KSA)**: Largest GCC economy. Vision 2030 drives massive digital transformation spending. IKTVA/local content requirements for government contracts. National champions preferred.
- **UAE**: Most business-friendly regulatory environment (DIFC, ADGM free zones). Strong fintech, logistics, and smart-city verticals. Gateway to broader MENA.
- **Qatar**: QIA-backed mega-projects (FIFA legacy, Lusail). Smaller but high-value deals. Strong focus on LNG-adjacent tech.
- **Bahrain**: Fintech hub (CBB sandbox). Lower cost base. Good pilot market before scaling to KSA/UAE.
- **Kuwait**: Conservative procurement. KIA sovereign fund. Oil & gas dominated.
- **Oman**: Vision 2040 diversification. Smaller deal sizes but less competition.

### 2. Map key stakeholders

**Sovereign Wealth Funds:**
- PIF (Saudi) — $930B+ AUM, invests across NEOM, entertainment, tech
- ADIA (Abu Dhabi) — $990B+ AUM, global diversified
- Mubadala (Abu Dhabi) — Tech-forward, semiconductor to AI
- QIA (Qatar) — $500B+ AUM, strategic sectors
- KIA (Kuwait) — $900B+ AUM, conservative allocation

**Family Offices & Conglomerates:**
- Olayan Group, SABIC affiliates, Al Futtaim, Majid Al Futtaim, Chalhoub Group, Al Ghurair, Kanoo Group
- Family offices increasingly co-invest with SWFs on tech deals

**Government Entities:**
- SDAIA, MCIT, NCA (Saudi cybersecurity), ZATCA (tax authority)
- Smart Dubai, ADDA (Abu Dhabi Digital Authority)
- Qatar MCIT, Bahrain EDB

### 3. Understand procurement and compliance

- **Saudization / Nitaqat**: Labor localization quotas. Tech companies may need Saudi employees or local partners.
- **IKTVA (In-Kingdom Total Value Add)**: For energy-sector contracts, a percentage of value must be created locally.
- **Data residency**: KSA PDPL requires personal data of Saudi residents to be processed locally or with adequacy. UAE has similar rules per sector.
- **Government procurement portals**: Etimad (KSA), TEJARI/Abu Dhabi procurement, Daman (Qatar).
- **Local entity**: Most B2G contracts require a CR (commercial registration) in-country. Free zone entities (e.g., DIFC, SAGIA) may suffice for B2B.

### 4. Build a soft-landing plan

1. **Validate demand** — Attend sector events (LEAP, GITEX, Qatar Economic Forum). Run 10-15 discovery calls with target buyers.
2. **Find a local partner** — Distributor, systems integrator, or sponsor. Mandatory for many government tenders.
3. **Set up entity** — Choose jurisdiction (mainland vs. free zone). Typical timeline: 2-6 weeks.
4. **Hire local** — At minimum a country manager with government relationships. Arabic-speaking BD is critical for B2G.
5. **Pilot first** — Land a lighthouse customer (often a semi-government entity). Use as reference for wider rollout.
6. **Localize product** — RTL Arabic UI, local payment methods (SADAD, Apple Pay MENA), Arabic support docs.

### 5. Funding and incentive programs

- **Saudi Venture Capital Company (SVC)**: Co-invests with VCs in KSA-based startups.
- **Monsha'at**: SME authority offering grants, incubation, and acceleration.
- **Hub71 (Abu Dhabi)**: Incentive program covering housing, cloud credits, and office space.
- **Bahrain FinTech Bay / Tamkeen**: Workforce subsidies and training grants.
- **Qatar Development Bank**: SME financing and startup support.
